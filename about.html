About

Watching Emeril Laggasse's cooking show is what inspired me to become a cook, and now a Head Chef

Around the age of 15 I when first dipped my toes in the culinary world. I world as a bar tender at a hotel, I used to make and serve drinks. At that time I watched a lot of cooking show on Food-Network, my favorite show at the time was from Emeril Lagasse. The way that he cooks was with a feeling and not by following a recipe. I liked this very much, because you cook by using your taste-buds and not by reading

When I started school, it was a sensory overload. I got to learn so much about produce and cooking. At that time I didn’t have a clue about how it would be to work in a kitchen or everything that is possible to do with food. My first kitchen experience was at Larousse in Curacao as an intern. At that moment it was a Michelline Start Restaurant, which I didn’t know. I spend my days there doing some mis en place and preparing the appetizers. Working at Larrouse wasn’t really a positive experience, the food lacked inspiration and love. There wasn’t much to do, because it as a high priced restaurant not allot of people would go there.

As I was looking for more challenges and things to do, I got send to Brakeput MeiMei in Curacao for my second intern. There is where I learned what it really meant to be a cook. It was a big restaurant, can sit about 200 guests at a time. The kitchen was made up of a 10 person team. Working here is where I learned how to work under pressure and that having a working routine is very important. After my intern I got hired to work there, I used to go to work after school for about a year

I got my ahha moment when I was working at Intermesszo in Curacao, I was working at head chef there and I had the freedom to do what I liked. They did at the time have their own menu, but I got the chance to come up with new ideas and make my own dishes. The review that I got there were so good, and I liked this very much. I was then and there that I knew what I wanted.

I move to The Netherlands in the 2010 to further my culinary studies and chase the dream of becoming a head chef at a restaurant. After 2 months being in The Netherlands I got my first job at Sluisje in Alkmaar. I liked working there because every day was different. There isn’t a time that I can think of that I did the same thing for days after each other. After a few months I started culinary school, I knew a lot already but the only problems was that I knew all cooking terms in English and now I needed to learn them in French. The most memorable moment of going to school, is when we got to go to France for a wine tour. We got to go different wineries, restaurant and escargot. It was a culinary eye-opening moment for me.

After a few years, and having to see different colleagues leave, I left Sluisje and landed a De Heren van Sonoy. De Heren van Sonoy I once again got the chance to come up with dishes. While working there I got also to be the Sous-Chef. This was a good experience, I liked that I was about to give direction to other cooks, and also have other cooks to help me achieve the things that I wanted.

A few years later, and some other working experience I ended up a Chefz in Oosterhout where I currently work. At Chefz I currently work as Head-Chef, they had really low expectation because all other previous chefs were underperforming. After 2 months of working there the owner and the customer liked the food that I was making. Then was where I official got the keys from the kitchen. I am now able to run the kitchen as I want to.

Until today I’m not allowed to quit because the owner is very happy with me and the customers loves my cooking.

About

Chris Harms is currently Head Chef at Chefz in Oosterhout, The Netherland. He has been cooking for over 10 years and would like to share some recipes with you.
